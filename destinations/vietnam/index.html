Accommodation in Vietnam

Budget: Hostels in Hanoi's Old Quarter and Hoi An's backpacker strip run $5–12/night for a dorm. Basic private rooms with fan cost $8–15. The quality is generally clean and social.

Mid-range: Private rooms with air-conditioning and ensuite bathroom in a mini-hotel or boutique guesthouse cost $18–50/night. Hoi An has exceptional boutique options at this price point with rice paddy views and breakfast included.

Comfort: International chain hotels and boutique resorts run $80–250+/night. For a splurge, the Nam Hai resort in Hoi An and Capella Hanoi represent the upper end of Vietnam's luxury market at $300–800/night.

Food and Drink Costs in Vietnam

Vietnamese street food is world-class and extraordinarily cheap. A bowl of pho from a street stall costs $1–2. Banh mi sandwiches run $0.80–1.50. A full meal at a local restaurant is $3–6.

Bia hoi — fresh draft beer brewed daily — costs as little as $0.25–0.50 per glass at street corners in Hanoi. Craft beer in a bar or Western-style restaurant runs $2–5. A bottle of local Tiger or Saigon beer at a restaurant is $1–2.

Eating at tourist-facing restaurants in Da Nang or Hoi An adds cost: $8–20 for a full meal. Fine dining at a Vietnamese-French fusion restaurant in Ho Chi Minh City costs $30–60 per person.

Getting Around Vietnam

Vietnam is a long, narrow country and transport adds up on a north-to-south itinerary. Budget options include overnight sleeper buses ($15–25 Hanoi to Hoi An) and local trains. Budget airlines like VietJet and Bamboo Airways offer Hanoi–Ho Chi Minh City flights from $30–60 if booked in advance — much faster than the 30-hour bus.

Within cities, Grab (the regional Uber) is reliable and cheap: most rides $1–3. Motorbike rental costs $5–15/day and is the most flexible way to explore rural areas and the central highlands. xe om (motorbike taxis) are available everywhere for $0.50–2/ride.

Activities and Experiences

Many of Vietnam's best experiences are very affordable. Wandering the ancient town of Hoi An at night is free. A cooking class runs $20–35. Ha Long Bay day cruise from Hanoi costs $80–120; overnight cruises on a junk boat start at $120–200.

Museum entry fees are $1–3. The War Remnants Museum in Ho Chi Minh City and the Ho Chi Minh Mausoleum in Hanoi are both under $3. Motorbike tours of the countryside run $25–50 per person.

Sample Vietnam Itinerary Budgets

1 week (solo budget traveler)

Hanoi 3 nights + Ha Long Bay overnight cruise + fly to Ho Chi Minh City for 2 nights: approx. $280–380 excluding flights. Includes dorm beds, street food, Grab rides, and the Ha Long cruise.

2 weeks (couple, mid-range)

Hanoi → Ha Long Bay → Hoi An → Ho Chi Minh City with private hotel rooms and day tours: $1,400–2,200 for two, excluding international flights. Internal Hanoi–Da Nang budget flight adds $60–80.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Is Vietnam cheap to travel?
    Yes. Vietnam is one of the most affordable destinations in Southeast Asia. Street food (pho, banh mi) costs $1–3, local beer from $0.50, and budget guesthouses from $8/night. A backpacker can comfortably travel on $25–35/day.
  • How much money do you need per day in Vietnam?
    Budget travelers spend $25–40/day. Mid-range travelers in private hotels with tours and restaurants spend $55–100/day. Comfortable travel with boutique hotels and private transfers costs $150+/day.
  • Is Vietnam cheaper than Thailand?
    Vietnam is slightly cheaper than Thailand, especially for food and accommodation. Street meals average $1–2 vs $1–3 in Thailand, and budget guesthouses start at $8 vs $10. Both countries offer excellent value overall.
  • What is the best time to visit Vietnam?
    Vietnam has three distinct climate zones. November–April is best for the south (Ho Chi Minh City, Mekong Delta) and central coast (Da Nang, Hoi An). June–August suits the north (Hanoi, Sapa, Ha Long Bay). The country is visitable year-round if you move between regions.
  • How much does 2 weeks in Vietnam cost for 2 people?
    Budget: $700–1,120 total. Mid-range: $1,540–2,800 total. Comfort: $4,200+. All figures exclude international flights. A north-to-south itinerary covering Hanoi, Ha Long Bay, Hoi An, and Ho Chi Minh City is very achievable in 14 days.
